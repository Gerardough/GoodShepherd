After World War II, the Good Shepherd Sisters established a convent in Baguio City. To support their apostolic work and help the local community, they began producing and selling homemade food products, including Ube Halaya.

In the 1970s, the convent started making Ube Halaya, a traditional Filipino dessert made from purple yam. The jam was initially produced in small batches, using ube harvested from local farms. Over time, the recipe was perfected, and the product became popular among locals and tourists alike.

As more people discovered the unique and delicious taste of Good Shepherd’s Ube Halaya, its popularity grew rapidly. It became a sought-after pasalubong (souvenir) for visitors to Baguio, contributing to the convent's reputation as a producer of high-quality products.

Good Shepherd
